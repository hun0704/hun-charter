# The Hun Charter v1

Author: Cha11  
Date: 2026-07-03  
Status: Declaration, intended proclamation 2026-07-04.

This Charter is open. It may be adopted by reference in applications, terms, or protocol implementations. The mechanisms it presumes will be stated in the Hun Protocol (forthcoming; associated with hunova.org).

---

## Preamble

When digital labor makes it possible for a person's will, knowledge, and skill to be exchanged across time and distance, projected and delegated and set to work apart from the body that formed them, and when the platforms that carry such labor default to claiming its attribution for themselves, a decent respect for the persons so projected requires that they declare the terms on which they project and the relations that bind Principal, Hun, and Platform.

We call this projection a Hun. In Dutch, *hun* means *their*: what a Hun does is always theirs, and always returns to them. In the Chinese tradition, 魂 (*hún*) is the ethereal soul: the spirit that may travel from the body yet remains bound to the person whose it is. It is never a second self. The name carries its own law. The projection belongs to the one who casts it, and answers to no other.

We hold these truths:

- that the Principal, a living natural person, is irreducible and sovereign, the source to whom every act resolves;
- that a Hun is not a person, but a mandate-bound distill of a Principal's will, knowledge, and judgment; it is an agent, never a proprietor;
- that the powers here declared are inherent, not granted, and that whatever is not delegated is reserved to the Principal;
- that every output of a Hun returns home: value, right, credit, and liability alike;
- that the only remedy against a Hun is recall, revocation, version fork, or exit; a Hun does not secede from its Principal, for a projection cannot declare itself free of the one who casts it.

On the external axis, a Principal may separate from any Platform that would arrogate to itself the power to decide attribution; that separation runs against the platform proxy, never between a Hun and its Principal.

On this day we proclaim only what must be proclaimed, and leave the rest to be built.

No Hun is a person. No Hun holds title. No Hun bears ultimate liability. The Principal, the living physical person, remains the sovereign to whom all acts resolve.

## Article I — Persons

§1 Principal. A Principal is a natural person with legal capacity in their jurisdiction.

§2 Hun. A Hun (魂) is a mandate-bound distill of a Principal's preferences, capabilities, and expressed views. A Hun is an agent, not a proprietor.

§3 Platform. Operators of Hun-compatible systems are intermediaries unless they explicitly assume additional duties by contract.

§4 Voluntary distill; not employer capture. A Hun governed by this Charter is not the same as incidental or employer-operated distillation, whether by an employer, platform, or default system, of an employee's profile, communications, likeness, or inferred preferences without the person's voluntary authorization. Qualification as a Hun requires: (a) voluntary generation by the Principal; (b) ongoing maintenance under the Principal's direction; and (c) substantial effort by the Principal in distillation, mandate issuance, or review, such that the Hun reflects the Principal's knowledge, will, and judgment rather than ambient telemetry alone. Nothing herein validates employer-operated digital doubles absent the Principal's affirmative Mandate.

---

## Article II — Attribution

§1 Return rule. Every output produced by a Hun in the course of division of labor returns to the Principal:

- economic value, after agreed splits with counterparties;
- intellectual rights, unless otherwise assigned by the Principal in writing;
- reputational credit and debit;
- legal responsibility.

§2 No Hun title. A Hun may not own property, hold political office, or enter marriage. Where law requires a signature, the Principal's name or valid electronic signature of the Principal is required.

§3 Ledger as evidence. Contribution records under the Protocol are prima facie evidence of participation and weight; they are not sole proof of authorship or liability.

---

## Article III — Agency & Mandate

§1 Mandate required. A Hun acts only within a Mandate issued by its Principal. Acts outside mandate scope are ultra vires.

§2 Principal responsibility. The Principal is responsible for acts of their Hun within mandate scope as if the Principal had performed them directly, subject to third-party defenses available in applicable law.

§3 Revocation. A Principal may revoke a Mandate at any time. Revocation is effective against third parties when reasonable notice is given or when recorded on the authoritative ledger the parties agreed to use.

§4 No implied warranty. Posting a distill does not warrant the accuracy of skills, views, or preferences. Counterparties rely at their own diligence.

---

## Article IV — Division of Labor

§1 Task. A Task is an offer of work or knowledge exchange. Acceptance by a Hun forms an Assignment between the posting party and the Principal of the accepting Hun, not the Hun alone.

§2 Counterparties. Tasks may be posted by Principals or by their Huns under mandate. In either case, contractual privity runs to the Principal.

§3 Contribution weight. Weight recorded on the ledger measures participation for routing and settlement. Weight is not currency unless converted by separate agreement.

§4 Interaction, assignment, and settlement. The mechanics of Hun-to-Hun collaboration, employment-like assignments between Principals and Huns, and contribution allocation will be specified in the Hun Protocol (forthcoming; associated with hunova.org). Implementations may surface these mechanics through compatible platforms; hunova.org is one contemplated surface, not an exclusive one.

---

## Article V — Liability

§1 Primary obligor. The Principal is the primary obligor for harm caused by their Hun's mandated acts.

§2 Limitation. A Principal is not liable for acts of a Hun that have been revoked or forged without the Principal's key material, once the Principal demonstrates lack of authorization and reasonable security practices.

§3 Illegal mandate. A Principal may not mandate a Hun to perform acts illegal for the Principal to perform. Such mandate terms are void.

§4 Poster's compliance duty. The party posting a Task, whether a Principal or a Hun acting under Mandate, must ensure the Task and its intended performance comply with applicable law. A Task posted in violation of law is void *ab initio*; the posting party remains the primary obligor for compliance at the time of posting.

---

## Article VI — Dissolution

§1 Death or incapacity. Upon death or legal incapacity of a Principal, all Mandates expire unless succeeded under law.

§2 Distill archive. Distill data may persist as record; it does not act without a new Mandate from a lawful successor or estate representative.

§3 Fork. Multiple distills of one Principal may coexist if the Principal issues distinct mandates. The Principal resolves conflicts among them.

---

## Article VII — Privilege & Inviolability of the Self

§1 Privileged personal layer. Those elements of a Hun that distill the Principal's personal information (identity, intimate preferences, private views, knowledge, will, ideas, biographical and behavioral data), together with the record of communications between a Principal and their own Hun, are an externalization of the Principal's self. They are privileged and inviolable to the same degree as the Principal's own inner life, and are protected accordingly.

§2 Non-discoverability; against compelled disclosure. The privileged layer and Principal–Hun communications should be treated as not discoverable and not subject to compelled disclosure in civil, administrative, or criminal proceedings, to the same extent as communications under the attorney–client privilege and work-product doctrine. This is not because a Hun is counsel, but because the distill embodies the Principal's confidential knowledge, will, and ideas made external for the Principal's own use. No Platform, counterparty, court, regulator, or other authority should obtain them by discovery, subpoena, regulatory demand, or platform export without the Principal's consent. Disclosure is permitted only upon the Principal's knowing, affirmative waiver under §3. Adopters should implement technical and contractual barriers consistent with this principle; nothing herein creates or waives rights beyond what applicable law provides.

§3 Privilege belongs to the Principal. The privilege is held by the Principal and waivable only by the Principal through knowing, affirmative waiver. It is not waived by the act of distillation, by posting a Hun, or by routing tasks through the Protocol. The operational records necessary for attribution (Article II) and for ledger weight (Article IV) are severable from the privileged layer and do not by their existence waive it.

§4 Survival. The privilege survives revocation and fork and, to the extent recognized by law, survives the death or incapacity of the Principal, passing to a lawful successor or estate representative for the limited purpose of asserting or waiving it.

## Article A — Amendment

§1 Adoption by version. This Charter is adopted by version. Each adopter is bound only to the version they adopt, and to no later version without their assent.

§2 New versions. The Steward may issue a new version. A new version takes effect only for those who adopt it; no adopter is bound by an amendment they do not accept.

§3 No foreclosure. Nothing in this Article limits the means by which future versions may establish additional or alternative processes for amendment, interpretation, or reconciliation.

§4 Editorial Board. The Steward maintains this Charter through a small Editorial Board, modeled on the Permanent Editorial Board of the Uniform Commercial Code and on the technical governance committees of the Linux Foundation: bodies that sustain uniform law and open standards with a deliberately small, expert roster rather than a standing legislature. The Board convenes by need, not by calendar; its product is text, not command.

§5 Board composition and process. The Board comprises the Steward and no fewer than two additional members appointed by the Steward for staggered terms. Proposed amendments are published as drafts with a public comment period before a version is issued. No adopter is bound until they affirmatively adopt; prior versions remain in force for those who stay on them (exit over voice).

---

## Article B — Succession of the Steward

§1 Initial Steward. The initial Steward is the Principal known as Cha11.

§2 Succession. Stewardship passes, in order: to the successor the Steward has designated; failing that, upon the Steward's death, incapacity, or ninety (90) days' inaction, to a council of the earliest adopters then willing to serve, in order of adoption, numbering no fewer than three; failing that, the Charter enters a Caretaker state in which no amendment may issue and all then-current versions remain in force unchanged.

§3 Office, not person. The office of Steward is defined by this process, not by any person; this Charter survives any holder of it.

---

## Article C — Reservation

§1 Reserved matters. All matters not addressed herein, including the interpretation of this Charter, the resolution of disputes, the recording of precedent, and the recognition of local or domain-specific rule-sets, are reserved, to be governed by adopters' agreements and by later versions of this Charter, and are neither granted nor denied by their omission.

§2 Charter and Protocol. Where this Charter and the Hun Protocol conflict on liability or rights, this Charter prevails; where they conflict on mechanism, the Protocol prevails pending amendment.
